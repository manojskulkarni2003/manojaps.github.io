4.11 Data transmission

Imagine a scenario where a user uploads an image or video to Facebook. Before transmission, Huffman coding compresses the file, reducing its size by assigning shorter codes to frequently occurring color values or pixels. This compressed data is then transmitted over the network, ensuring efficient use of bandwidth and faster data transfer speeds. In essence, Huffman coding plays a crucial role in optimizing data transmission within Facebook, contributing to a smoother and more efficient user experience across various forms of content shared on the platform
